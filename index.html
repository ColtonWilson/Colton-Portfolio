About Me

In college, I was blessed with the opportunity tp choose between two universities. One path where I could follow my brain and get a bachelor's in Computer Science and another will I could follow my heart and be a member of an NCAA Men's Swim team but have to get a degree in Applied Mathematics since they did not offer any Computer Science or Engineering programs. I choose the latter and decided that not many people get the opportunity to compete on a collegiate level and I could always further my education with Computer Science.
After College I found myself more in need of financial support than taking on more debt. I was recruited by some companies and decided it was in my best interest to work for a few years to build up a savings. After about five years, I found myself being very successful and was the youngest manager in the company. I did not want to start over and so y dreams of going back to school were a distant thought.
Then in 2020 the world saw a life-changing pandemic and the line of work I was in was not sure if they would stay open. I was offered a chance to leave and I took it as a sign to go and do something else. I started taking some night classes where I learned HTML, CSS, and JavaScript but it still wasn't scratching my need to learn. I started looking at some schools and I found Syracuse University had the best alignment for what I was looking for in a school. For the next two years I found myself working part-time while being full-time with getting my Master's in Computer Science. I learned a lot of new languages like C++, Python, and SQL and some great topics like computer and network security, data mining, and object oriented programming.
I currently work as a data analyst in digital products for Target and am already beginning to learn so much more than I could have imagined. I enjoy learning and continue to take classes to improve my front-end programming. I would like to continue learning and adding more languages and techniques to my tool-kit.
